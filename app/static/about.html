The Anecdote

When Mozart played The Abduction from the Seraglio in 1782 for Emperor Joseph II, Joseph supposedly told him there were "too many notes." Mozart's legendary reply: "There are just as many notes as there should be, no more, no less."

The emperor couldn't answer. I suppose he had no specific critique—just a vague sense that something was "too much."

⚡ What Joseph Stands For

  • Transparency over obfuscation. Every metric is shown. You see the ML model score, the entropy analysis, the perplexity, the burstiness—all of it.
  • Skepticism over certainty. Joseph doesn't claim perfect accuracy. It acknowledges its limitations. False positives happen. AI detection is probabilistic, not forensic, nor evidencial.
  • Open over closed. The code is public. The model is downloadable. You can fork it, audit it, break it, improve it.
  • Tools, not judges. Joseph doesn't tell you what to believe. It shows you data and lets you think for yourself.

What Joseph Is NOT

Joseph is not evidence. It's not a lie detector. It's not a plagiarism checker. It's not a substitute for actually reading someone's work.

It's a tool. A flawed, experimental, alpha-stage tool. Use it accordingly.

Joseph is open-source, MIT licensed, and can run 100% locally.
